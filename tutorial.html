Choosing songs

Choose two songs with no more then 6 BPM difference and load them into your turntables or software.
BPM = Beats Per Minutes

Volumes and play

Put the left volume slide at 100%.
Put the right volume slide at 0%.
Make sure the left and right bass knobs are at 100%

Syncronizing beats

Make sure to press BEAT SYNC on the track you want mixed in.

Put on your headphones

On the mixertable you will find headphone MASTER and two buttons, CUE 1 and CUE 2.
CUE 1 and 2 represent the left and right song playing in your headphones.
For this tutorial you will press down CUE 2.

What does bar mean?

In dance music there is a basskick beating.
Four of those beats forms a bar.
Usually after 32 beats or 8 bars something will happen in a song. Keep this in mid.

First Kickdrum

Navigate to the start of the song you are mixing in and spin the turntable untill you hear the first kickdrum.
Now hold PLAY and press CUE and release. You have now saved that starting point to your CUE and PLAY button.
Now if you press QUE it will start from the first kickdrum. Great!

CUE button

The CUE button work like the play button except if you release it the music stops.
Tapp the CUE button on the right turntable with the rythmn of the left songs kickdrum.
When it's time to play the song of the right, switch tapping the cue button to push the play button instead.
Use the outer part of the wheel on the turntables to adjust the last percent if needed. beatmatch the two songs together to make them sound clean and avoid noise cancelation.

When should I press PLAY?

After the breakdown and 16 bars into the Outro it's time to press PLAY.
Remember 1 bar = 4 beats.

Volumes

Soon its time to mix the two songs together so the crowd can hear too!
But first, start with by turning down the right bass knob to 0%.
Slowly turn up the volume of the right track to 50% within 16 to 32 bars of the outro.

Mixing

At 32 bars into the Outro there will be another small drop.
When the drop hits turn up the volume to 80% and the right bass knob up to 50%.
Use the next 8 to 16 bar to increase the volume to 100%.

The final

48 bars into the outro the is another drop. switch the two bass knobs so the right goes to 100% and the left goes to 0%. use the next 8 to 16 bars to turn down the volume of the left track to 0%.
Congratulations you have completed your first mix!
